Foods

Sinigang

A classic Filipino soup known for its distinct sourness, usually from tamarind, balanced with the savory flavors of pork, shrimp, or fish. It is loaded with vegetables like kangkong, tomatoes, and radish, making it both hearty and nutritious. The tangy broth makes it a perfect comfort food, especially on rainy days.

Bulalo

A rich and comforting beef soup made by slow-cooking beef shank and bone marrow until tender, creating a flavorful broth. It is often paired with corn, cabbage, and potatoes, adding sweetness and texture to the dish. Best enjoyed hot, bulalo is a favorite in colder regions like Tagaytay.

Sisig

A sizzling Filipino dish made from finely chopped pork face, ears, and liver, mixed with onions, chili, and a tangy calamansi-based seasoning. It is traditionally served on a hot plate, often topped with a raw egg that cooks in the residual heat. This crispy, savory, and slightly spicy dish is a popular beer companion and street food favorite.

Adobo

A well-loved Filipino dish made by braising meat—commonly chicken or pork—in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. The slow cooking process results in tender, flavorful meat with a balance of salty, tangy, and slightly sweet flavors. Known for its versatility and long shelf life, adobo is a staple in Filipino households.

Lechon Belly

A boneless, rolled version of traditional lechon, slow-roasted to achieve crispy, golden-brown skin while keeping the meat tender and flavorful. It is marinated with spices like garlic, lemongrass, and salt. Often served at celebrations, it pairs perfectly with liver sauce, spiced vinegar, or soy sauce with calamansi for an extra burst of flavor.

Kare-Kare

A rich and creamy Filipino stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables, simmered in a thick peanut sauce. The nutty and mildly sweet flavor pairs perfectly with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste), which adds a salty contrast. Kare-kare is a well-loved dish served during special occasions and family gatherings.
